WEAVER, Better, Faster, Longer: An Effective World Model for Robotic Manipulation

Arnav Kumar Jain*, Yilin Wu*, Jesse Farebrother, Gokul Swamy, Andrea Bajcsy

Mila - Quebec AI Institute, Universite de Montreal, McGill University, Carnegie Mellon University

arXiv Paper Code Data

We introduce WEAVER: a world model architecture that satisfies the three desiderata: (i) fidelity, (ii) consistency, and (iii) efficiency. WEAVER unlocks state-of-the-art performance across policy evaluation (ρ = 0.870 correlation with real-world success rate), policy improvement (real-world success rate improvement of 38% on top of the π0.5 robot foundation model), and test-time planning (real-world success rate improvement of 14% with a 5–10× speedup over prior WMs).

Fidelity

WEAVER predicts both external and wrist-camera observations, preserving the views used by modern visuomotor robot policies.

Consistency

Sparse memory plus short-term history helps the model remain coherent as objects become occluded or leave and re-enter view.

Efficiency

Reward and critic heads operate directly on imagined latents, avoiding expensive decoding and external judge calls during planning.

Method

WEAVER is an action-conditioned latent world model for long-horizon robot manipulation. It predicts future latent states, decodes future observations when needed, and plans in latent space. The key design decisions are:

Architecture and design. WEAVER predicts multiple views and robot proprioceptive state. The sparse long-term memory preserves scene context across occlusions, short-term history captures recent motion, and an efficient spatio-temporal Transformer generates future latents conditioned on the action tokens.

Training and fast inference. The latent dynamics model is trained with flow matching and Diffusion Forcing, which supports consistent long-horizon prediction with different noise levels across future steps. At inference time, KV caching and rectified-flow post-training reduce the cost for planning.

Accurate reward and value estimation. To efficiently score action chunks without decoding latents into images or querying an external VLM judge, WEAVER uses a lightweight reward head that operates directly on imagined latent states and the language instruction. A critic estimates returns beyond the imagined horizon.

World Model Evaluation

Compared with Ctrl-World, WEAVER generates higher-fidelity rollouts under lower inference budgets and preserves quality over longer horizons. The paper reports lower FID/FVD on DROID validation and out-of-distribution task data while using less inference time.

Latent Verifier and Planning

WEAVER's latent reward estimates match RoboMeter reward trends on held-out real trajectories and can distinguish action samples by predicted advantage.

Downstream applications

The paper evaluates five real-world manipulation tasks: Stack Bowls, PnP Bag, PnP Marker, PnP Towel, and Pour Beans. WEAVER supports three downstream applications: policy evaluation, finetuning policies inside the world model, and test-time steering.

Policy Evaluation

WEAVER can act as a learned simulator for offline policy evaluation: imagined success rates track real-world success rates across policies and tasks, with the fine-tuned model reaching rho = 0.870.

Finetuning Policy

Finetuning on synthetic segments generated by WEAVER closely matches real-data finetuning, while combining real and synthetic segments performs best.

Test-time Steering

Advantage-based steering samples candidate action chunks in WEAVER, scores imagined futures, and selects actions with better predicted outcomes.
